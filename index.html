Valtellina Explored

Discoveries in Nature, Culture, and Pleasure

Iconic Landscapes

Discover the breathtaking vistas of Valtellina, from the majestic peaks of the Stelvio National Park to the serene beauty of Lake Como's northern shores. Each view tells a story of nature's grandeur.

Gastronomic Delights

Indulge in the rich flavors of Valtellina's cuisine. Experience the traditional pizzoccheri, bresaola, and Sciatt, accompanied by the exquisite notes of Nebbiolo wines.

Vineyard Tours

Traverse the terraced vineyards that adorn the mountain slopes. Learn about the ancient art of winemaking in Valtellina and savor the taste of its world-renowned wines.

Cultural Heritage

Explore the deep-rooted culture and history of Valtellina. From age-old castles and churches to lively festivals, uncover the stories that have shaped this enchanting valley.

Outdoor Adventures

Whether it's hiking the scenic trails, skiing the snowy slopes, or cycling along the river, Valtellina offers a plethora of outdoor activities for every thrill-seeker.

Wildlife Encounters

Get up close with the diverse wildlife that calls Valtellina home. Spot deer, foxes, and a variety of birds in their natural habitat as you explore the valley.

Local Crafts

Discover the craftsmanship of Valtellina through its local products. From intricately woven textiles to handcrafted wooden artifacts, each piece tells a story of tradition and artistry.

Wellness Retreats

Unwind in the tranquility of Valtellina's spa towns. Natural thermal waters, surrounded by stunning landscapes, offer a perfect escape for relaxation and rejuvenation.

Architectural Wonders

Marvel at the architectural beauty of Valtellina, from the Romanesque simplicity of its ancient churches to the elegance of its noble palaces.

Valtellina's Wild Heart

Chamois

A type of goat-antelope, chamois are well-adapted to living in the mountainous terrain of Valtellina

Red Deer

These majestic creatures are among the largest deer species and can be spotted in forested areas of Valtellina

Golden Eagle

This magnificent bird of prey is one of the most emblematic species of the Alpine region, often seen soaring above the mountains

Marmot

These large ground squirrels are a common sight in Alpine meadows, known for their burrowing habits and loud calls

European Adder

This venomous snake is found in a variety of habitats including forests, meadows, and rocky slopes

Alpine Salamander

Unique to the Alpine region, this salamander is black, lives at high altitudes, and is viviparous, giving birth to fully formed young
